Kinship

A proposal for a different way of living

We're living through a great unraveling. The systems supporting the web of life are fraying under pressure from a civilization fundamentally inconsistent with the environment that nourishes it. Many of us sense this divergence and feel called to explore different ways of living.

Kinship is my invitation to journey together—seeking others willing to explore what it might mean to live according to the Seven Laws of Ecology, guided by three fundamental freedoms: to move, to disobey, and to create new social worlds.

This is not a political project or utopian blueprint. It's an ecological inquiry—asking what it means to live in alignment with how life actually works, observing natural processes, letting ecological laws guide us rather than human ideological frameworks.

What Makes This Different

A Shift in Perception, Not a Method

I'm not proposing any single body of knowledge, technique, or method as "the solution." No ideology guides this. No system provides the answers. What I'm proposing is a shift in perception—a different way of seeing our relationship with all life—and letting everything else flow from that.

The Seven Laws of Ecology aren't prescriptions for how to live. They're a lens for understanding how life works. When we see through this lens, when we recognize ourselves as kin to land, water, animals, plants—to the whole community of life—different choices become possible. This is about a way of being, not a method to follow.

The Spirit of This Exploration

This is for those who hear "primitive" as a compliment rather than an insult—who see living close to the land, using simple tools, meeting needs directly as aspiration rather than deprivation. If that resonates, you might be called to this path.
